Creating Email Filters For Squadbox


If you use Gmail (or Google Inbox):

Go to this page to authorize Squadbox to access your Gmail account. We will use this access to:

  • Import your contacts and people you've recently emailed to generate whitelist suggestions
  • Create a filter that automatically forwards a copy of messages you want moderated to Squadbox
  • Hide the messages that are waiting for moderation by temporarily moving them to the trash, and then move them back to your inbox when they're approved

If you don't use Gmail:

Note: if you have experience with email clients besides Gmail or have successfully set up Squadbox with a non-Gmail client, we would love to hear any tips or info from you on that process, and would welcome contributions to this guide! Check out our GitHub issue here.

What we are trying to accomplish here is, in words: "if an email needs moderation and has not already been to Squadbox, send it to Squadbox."
This means that we must slightly modify messages after they pass through Squadbox so that your email client can tell the difference and not keep sending the same messages back to Squadbox over and over.
The way we prevent this is by adding a secret extra piece of information to messages, called a "list ID". (This is usually set by messages sent via a mailing list). Your email client can look for that secret and know that emails containing it are approved. The custom list ID we will set on messages that have been approved is: {{filter_hash}}.

You will need to create a filter that says: "if a message matches my moderation criteria* and it did not come from the mailing list/have list-id {{filter_hash}}, forward it to {{group_address}} and move it to my trash/some non-inbox folder."

*What is moderation criteria? It defines messages you want to pass through Squadbox - i.e. messages from certain senders, or containing certain words, or with a particular subject line, etc. You can get creative as your email client's filters allow. Note: if messages from whitelisted senders get forwarded to Squadbox, they will be automatically approved and sent back immediately.
